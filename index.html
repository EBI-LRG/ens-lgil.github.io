Advantages of using LRG sequences
  • provide a unique stable sequence record with an ID and no versioning
  • provide a coordinate system for permanent and unambiguous reporting of disease-causing variants
  • are independent of updates to the reference genome assembly
  • are important for bidirectional data exchange and integration
  • are compiled and maintained by the EBI and NCBI and so will be maintained in perpetuity
  • are integrated into the Ensembl and NCBI genome browsers to benefit from visualisation and existing annotation
  • can be used as the reference sequence for HGVS nomenclature so that variant descriptions remain stable
  • support legacy exon and amino acid numbering schemes
  • are supported by the Mutalyzer sequence variant nomenclature checker
